BASE LAYERS
Turn on more than one to stack them — aerial sits at the bottom, topo on top.
Combines OSM roads/trails with elevation contours and hillshading — works well in both wilderness and urban areas. Not eligible for the offline area downloader (community-funded tile policy), but caches automatically as you browse.
USFS Forest Service topo — green tinted, shows section lines, USFS roads, and ranger district detail. Best for NF land navigation. Combine with World Hillshade for the full effect.
LiDAR-derived where available. Good for spotting old roadbeds under cover. Best at partial opacity over topo or aerial.
USGS aerial + topo labels baked in as one layer.
OVERLAYS
USGS rivers, streams & lakes — clean overlay over imagery where water is hard to pick out.
Esri World Hillshade Dark — adds terrain shadow depth to any base map. Use with Forest Service topo or USGS topo at 60–70% for the blended effect shown on usfs.maps.arcgis.com.
Loads live from a federal server; may lag. Only covers National Forest land.
Color-coded by agency (BLM, USFS, NPS, state, etc.) — nationwide BLM Surface Management Agency data.
Current fire perimeters from NIFC, updated daily. Season: roughly May–Nov.
NOAA daily snow analysis — useful for access planning Nov–Apr.
Bortle scale — dark areas are best for astrophotography. Black/grey=excellent, blue/green=good, yellow/red=poor.
USGS National Land Cover Database — habitat types: forests, shrublands, wetlands, croplands.
